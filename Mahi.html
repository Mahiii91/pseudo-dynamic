About the Achaemenid Ceremonial Complex

Pasargad and Persepolis were both essential royal sites in ancient Iran. Pasargad was the first imperial capital built by Cyrus the Great, and Persepolis (Takht-e Jamshid) became the grand ceremonial center for his successors. The buildings were used for royal receptions, tribute ceremonies, worship, and the display of Persian power.

Real building use: kings met foreign delegations, priests walked in processions, and officials brought gifts to the Great King. The site was a stage for the Achaemenid Empire.
